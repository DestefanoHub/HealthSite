Abrasions

When To Call 911

  • The victim has suffered an avulsion (a large portion of skin and tissue has been removed).
  • Bleeding continues several minutes after applying pressure or a bandage.
  • The victim has not had a tetanus vaccination in the past 5 years and the wound was exposed to foreign particles.
  • Signs of infection.

What To Do

  1. Wash the wound with soap and water, removing any foreign particles in the wound as well.
  2. Stop any bleeding by applying pressure to the wound.
  3. Bandage the wound.
  4. Apply an ice pack if the person is in pain.
  5. Continually check the wound for signs of infection:
    • Continuing pain.
    • The wound becoming red and swollen.
    • Pus.

What Not To Do

  • Use a cleaning agent such as:
    • Peroxide
    • Medical alcohol
    • Iodine
