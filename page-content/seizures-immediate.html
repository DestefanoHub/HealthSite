Seizures

What To Do

Warning Signs of a Possible Seizure

  • Odd feelings, often indescribable
  • Unusual smells, tastes, or feelings
  • Unusual experiences – 'out-of-body' sensations; feeling detached; body looks or feels different; situations or people look unexpectedly familiar or strange
  • Feeling spacey, 'fuzzy', or confused
  • Periods of forgetfulness or memory lapses
  • Daydreaming episodes
  • Jerking movements of an arm, leg, or body
  • Falling
  • Tingling, numbness or feelings of electricity in part of the body
  • Headaches
  • Unexplained confusion, sleepiness, weakness
  • Losing control of urine or stool unexpectedly

Treatment During a Seizure:

  1. Stay calm.
  2. Protect the person from injury.
  3. Keep them from falling or guide them to the floor
  4. Move furniture or other objects.
  5. Put them on their side so that the fluid from their mouth can leak out.
  6. Pay attention to what the person is doing to tell the doctors later:
    • How the person's body moved
    • How long the seizure lasted
    • How the person acted before the seizure
    • How the person acted immediately after the seizure
    • Whether the person suffered any injuries from the seizure

Treatment After a Seizure:

  1. Check person for injuries
  2. If person is not on their side turn them on it
  3. If person is having trouble breathing clear airway with finger
  4. Loosen clocks around neck and waist
  5. Provide safe area for rest
  6. Do not let person eat or drink until they are fully alert.
  7. Stay with the person until surroundings are familiar

What Not To Do

    During the Seizure...
    • But don’t apply too much pressure on the body.
    • Don’t put fingers in their mouth.
    • Don’t try to hold the person down.

When To Call 911

  • The person has never had a seizure before.
  • The person has difficulty breathing or waking after the seizure.
  • The seizure lasts longer than 5 minutes.
  • The person has another seizure soon after the first one.
  • The person is hurt during the seizure.
  • The seizure happens in water.
  • The person has a health condition like diabetes, heart disease, or is pregnant.
