Burns

What To Do

Symptoms to recognize:

    1st Degree:
    • redness
    • minor inflammation (swelling)
    • pain
    • dry, peeling skin (occurs as the burn heals)
    2nd Degree:
    • The skin can blister
    • Extremely red
    • Sore
    • Some blisters pop and open, giving the burn a wet appearance.
    3rd Degree:
    • waxy and white
    • charred dark brown
    • raised and leathery

After Checking the Scene and the Person for Injury

    Follow these steps:
    1. Stop the Burn
        2. Remove the source of the burn.
    2. Cool the Burn
        3. Run the burned area under cold water for about five minutes Or until the pain is relieved.
    3. Cover the Burn
        4. Cover the burn with loose STERILE gauze.
    Also be aware that if the burn is severe enough a person can go into shock.

What Not To Do

  • NEVER use ICE on a burn no matter the degree. It can cause farther damage.
  • DO NOT use COTTON BALLS on a burn wound.

When To Call 911

  • If this a 3rd Degree burn please seek professional assistance.
  • While waiting for medical assistance...
    • Raise the injury above heart.
    • Make sure no clothing is stuck to burn.
  • Seek Emergency Medical Treatment if there are 2nd Degree Burns on your face, your hands, your buttocks, your groins, and your feet.
