Burns

When To Call 911

  • If the vicitm suffered a 3rd degree burn
  • While waiting for medical assistance:
    • Raise the injury above heart.
    • Make sure no clothing is stuck to burn.
  • If the victim has 2nd degree burns on their face, hands, buttocks, groin, or feet.

What To Do

Symptoms:

  • 1st Degree
    • Redness.
    • Minor inflammation (swelling).
    • Pain.
    • Dry, peeling skin (occurs as the burn heals).
  • 2nd Degree
    • Blisters.
    • Extremely red skin.
    • Soreness around injury.
    • Some blisters pop giving the burn a wet appearance.
  • 3rd Degree
    • The skin appears:
      • Waxy and white.
      • Charred dark brown.
      • Raised and leathery.

After checking the scene and the person for injury:

  1. Stop the burn.
  2. Run the burned area under cold water for about five minutes or until the pain is relieved.
  3. Cover the burn with loose sterile gauze.
  4. Also be aware that if the burn is severe enough a person can go into shock.
  5. If the person suffered a 2nd degree burn, bring them to a physician.

What Not To Do

  • Use ice on a burn no matter the degree. It can cause further damage.
  • Use cotton balls on a burn wound.

Dr Rock Says: Remember to Call 911 if this is an emergency!
