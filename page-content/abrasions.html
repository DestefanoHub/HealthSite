Abrasions

  • Causes
  • Treatment
  • Quiz

Commonly known as scrapes, abrasions are injuries where the skin is rubbed or torn off. It is likely that most people have suffered an abrasion before, likely multiple times. Common abrasions are rug burn, and skinning your knee or elbow. Abrasions are not typically deep and usually do not bleed much, if at all. However, as with most injuries, abrasions to the face or other blood rich areas are likely to bleed, which can be misleading in terms of severity. It is common to receive abrasions more often on bony or thinly skinned areas, like the elbows, knees, hands, and shins. Abrasions are typically more painful than lacerations or puncture wounds because a much larger surface area is affected.

While most abrasions are not deep or serious, an avulsion is a serious type of abrasion where the affected skin and tissue is actually removed. These are very severe wounds because the affected region is torn off, so it is hard to repair the damage. An avulsion should be cared for by a medical professional immediately.

The first step in treatment is to clean the wound as particles can get into the wound easily. However, it is likely that the wound is not deep and any particles can usually be removed with ease by washing the wound with soap and water. It is not recommended to use alcohol, iodine, peroxide, or other such cleaning agents as it could further damage tissue and slow down healing. Next, stop any bleeding by simply applying pressure to the wound and by elevating it above the heart. This can be more difficult than for other injuries because of the larger surface area of the wound. Finally, apply a bandage to the wound. Applying an ice pack would help to lessen any pain.

Infections are possible with abrasions; pay attention for continuing pain, the wound becoming red and swollen, and pus. If bleeding has not ceased after several minutes of applying pressure or a bandage, seek medical attention. If the victim has not had a tetanus vaccination in the past five years and the wound has been exposed to foreign materials, help should be sought immediately. If someone has suffered an avulsion, immediately bring them to the hospital.
