Allergic Reactions

What To Do

    Signs of a Severe Allergic Reactions

    • abdominal cramping or pain
    • pain or tightness in the chest
    • diarrhea
    • difficulty swallowing
    • dizziness (vertigo)
    • fear or anxiety
    • flushing of the face
    • nausea or vomiting
    • heart palpitations
    • swelling of the face, eyes, or tongue
    • weakness
    • wheezing
    • difficulty breathing
    • unconsciousness

    Signs of a Life-Threatening Allergic Reactions

    • swelling of the airway
    • inability to breathe
    • sudden and severe drop in blood pressure

    Treatment of Allergic Reaction

      If a person is showing any of the serious symptoms of a Severe Allergic Reaction or Life-Threatening Allergic Reaction:
      • Check if the person is breathing if they are not administer CPR
      • Ask or Check if the person has an EpiPen. If they do help them administer the Epipen
      If the person is unconscious:
      • lay the person flat on their back and elevate their legs.
      • place a blanket over them. This will prevent the person from going into shock.

      How to Administer an EpiPen:

      1. Remove EpiPen from the Carrie Tube
      2. Grasp the Epipen with the ORANGE TIP facing downwards.
      3. Pull of the blue safety release on the top of the EpiPen
      4. Align Orange Tip near outer thigh.
      5. Pull arm back and swing at 90 degree angle, the EpiPen back into your thigh.
      6. Push the EpiPen into the thigh until you hear the EpiPen click.
      7. NOTE: EpiPen is suppose to work through clothing.
      8. Hold the EpiPen against the thigh for 10 seconds.
      9. Remove EpiPen
      10. Massage injection site for 10 seconds.
      11. Get Emergency Assistance.
      12. Put EpiPen back in Case and take it with you to the hospital

      How to Perform CPR:

      1. Give 30 Chest Compressions
      2. Give 2 Rescue Breaths
      3. Do not stop

What Not To Do

When To Call 911

  • If the person experiencing the allergic reaction is showing some of the more serious symptoms of a Severe Allergic Reaction such as swelling of the face, eyes, or tongue, call 911 or get the person to emergency facility.
  • If any of the Life-Threatening Allergic Reaction Symptoms occur PLEASE CALL 911 IMMEDIATELY, Without Treatment A Person Can Die Within 15 Minutes.
