Lacerations

When To Call 911

  • Deep lacerations, especially where there is internal damage, or when it is near a vital area.
  • Bleeding continues several minutes after applying pressure or a bandage.
  • The victim has not had a tetanus vaccination in the past 5 years and the wound was exposed to foreign particles.
  • Signs of infection.

What To Do

  1. Wash the wound with soap and water, removing any foreign particles in the wound as well.
  2. Attempt to remove large foreign particles.
  3. Stop any bleeding by applying pressure to the wound.
  4. Bandage the wound.
  5. Apply an ice pack if the person is in pain.
  6. Continually check the wound for signs of infection:
    • Continuing pain.
    • The wound becoming red and swollen.
    • Pus.
  7. It may be necessary to visit a physician to get stitches depending on the size and depth of the wound.

What Not To Do

  • Use a cleaning agent such as:
    • Peroxide
    • Medical alcohol
    • Iodine
  • Apply a tourniquet only if the wound is serious.

Dr Rock Says: Remember to Call 911 if this is an emergency!
