911 Call

  • Take a deep breath.
  • Call 911.
  • What to tell the 911 operator
    • Give the operator your name.
    • The location of the emergency including the street name.
    • The phone number you are calling from.
    • The reason you are calling.
    • A physical description of the injured person(s).
    • Describe the injuries or symptoms of the person needing medical assistance.
    • Listen to the operator's instructions.
    • Answer all of their questions.
    • Stay on the the line until the operator instructs you to hang up.

Remember, the 911 operator will instruct you while you are on the phone. Always follow all of the operator's instructions. Also, make sure to always call 911 first in an emergency. The information presented above is just useful to have when calling 911 so you can more quickly describe the situation to the operator. Do not wait to call 911 so that you can gather all of the above information.

Remember to Call 911 if this is an emergency!
